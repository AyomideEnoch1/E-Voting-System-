Process Overview

How voting works

From registration to receipt — here's everything you need to know about casting your secure ballot.

Step 01

Register

Create an account using your official @run.edu.ng school email. Only RUN students and staff are eligible to participate.

Step 02

Verify Email

Click the verification link sent to your inbox. Once verified, your unique Voter ID (VTR-XXXXXX) is emailed to you automatically — keep it safe.

Step 03

Cast Your Vote

Sign in during an active election window. Browse the candidates, make your selection, enter your Voter ID, and submit your encrypted ballot.

Step 04

Get Your Receipt

Immediately receive a cryptographic receipt hash. Save it — you can use it at any time to verify that your ballot was counted in the final tally.

About Your Voter ID

Your Voter ID (format: VTR-XXXXXX) is sent to your school email once you verify your account. It acts as a second authentication factor when submitting your ballot — preventing impersonation even if your login credentials are compromised. Do not share your Voter ID with anyone. If you lose it, go to My Account → Request Voter ID Reset and an administrator will issue a new one.

Two-factor verified

Trust Architecture

Built on cryptographic trust

Every component of SecureVote is designed to ensure that votes are secret, tamper-proof, and publicly verifiable.

Ballot Encryption

Every vote is encrypted client-side before transmission using industry-standard symmetric encryption. Your choice is never sent in plaintext.

AES-256-GCM
Digital Signatures

Election results are digitally signed with the administrator's private key after tallying. Any tampering with results is cryptographically detectable.

RSA-4096 / ECDSA
Password Hashing

Passwords are never stored in plaintext. We use adaptive hashing with a high work factor so that even in a data breach, passwords cannot be easily recovered.

bcrypt · high cost
Voter Anonymity

Votes are stored detached from voter identity. The link between a voter and their ballot is severed at submission — ensuring your choice remains private.

Anonymous ballots
Verifiable Receipts

After voting you receive a unique cryptographic hash. At any time you can submit this hash to verify your vote was included — without revealing who you voted for.

SHA-256 receipt hash
Audit Log

Every system action — logins, votes, admin operations — is recorded in an immutable audit trail with hashed IP addresses. Full accountability at every level.

Immutable audit log

Voter Guide

Everything you need to vote

A complete walkthrough of the voting experience from registration to verifying your receipt.

Before Election
Register with your school email

Visit /register and fill in your full name and @run.edu.ng email. No other accounts or logins are accepted. You'll need access to your school inbox throughout this process.

Within Minutes
Verify your email address

Check your school inbox for a verification email from SecureVote. Click the link inside — it expires in 24 hours. If it doesn't arrive, check your spam folder.

After Verification
Receive and save your Voter ID

A second email will deliver your unique Voter ID (e.g. VTR-482901). Save this somewhere safe — you will need it every time you cast a vote. It does not expire.

During Election Window
Sign in and cast your ballot

Go to /login, enter your credentials, then visit the Elections tab. Click Vote Now, read each candidate's profile, make your selection, and confirm with your Voter ID.

After Voting
Copy your receipt hash

A receipt modal will appear after your vote is accepted. Copy the hash string — you can use it in the Verify Receipt tab at any time to confirm your ballot was counted.

What you need to vote
A valid @run.edu.ng email address
A verified SecureVote account
Your Voter ID (emailed after verification)
An active election in the voting window
An internet-connected device (desktop, phone, tablet)
Rules & restrictions
Each voter may vote once per election — no exceptions
Votes cannot be changed or retracted after submission
Do not share your Voter ID or login credentials
Only @run.edu.ng accounts are accepted
Attempting to vote multiple times will be flagged in the audit log
Need help?
Lost your Voter ID? Go to My Account → Request Voter ID Reset
Forgot your password? Use Forgot Password on the sign-in page
Email not verified? Check spam, or re-request verification
Technical issues? Contact the election administrator via the official RUN channels

FAQ

Common questions

After you register and verify your email address, a second automated email is sent to your school inbox containing your unique Voter ID in the format VTR-XXXXXX. This happens automatically within seconds of verification. If you don't see it, check your spam/junk folder. If it still hasn't arrived after a few minutes, contact the system administrator.
No. Once a vote is submitted and encrypted on the system, it is final and cannot be changed or retracted. This is by design — the integrity of the election depends on the immutability of each ballot. Make sure you've reviewed your candidate selection carefully before confirming.
Yes. SecureVote is designed so that once your vote is submitted, the link between your identity and your ballot choice is severed. Votes are stored in an anonymised form — even administrators cannot see which candidate a specific voter chose. You can verify your vote was counted using your receipt hash, but no one can identify how you voted.
After submitting your vote you'll receive a receipt hash — a long string of characters unique to your ballot. You can paste this into the Verify Receipt section of the voter portal at any time to confirm your ballot was included in the election tally. It does not reveal who you voted for, only that your vote was recorded and counted.
Sign in to the voter portal, go to My Account, and scroll to the Request a Voter ID Reset section. Fill in a brief reason and submit the request. An administrator will review it and issue a new Voter ID — your old one will be invalidated automatically.
You can vote during an election's active window — the specific start and end dates are shown on each election card in the voter portal. Elections are managed by university administrators. You'll be able to see upcoming, active, and past elections from your dashboard once you're logged in.
After an election closes, administrators trigger the tally process. All votes are decrypted, counted, and the final results are digitally signed with a cryptographic key. The voter portal displays whether the signature is valid — any tampering with results after signing would break the signature and be immediately visible to all voters.
